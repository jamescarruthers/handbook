    1The FCA has been designated as the UK competent authority for the purpose of the benchmarks regulation.

  2. (2)

    The benchmarks regulation confers various directly applicable powers on the FCA2 in relation to critical benchmarks. In particular:

    1. (a)

      article 21(3) of the benchmarks regulation gives a the FCA2 the power to compel the administrator of a critical benchmark to continue publishing the critical benchmark for up to 24 months; and

    2. (b)

      article 23(6) of the benchmarks regulation gives a the FCA2 the power to take various steps where it considers that the representativeness of a critical benchmark is put at risk. That includes the power to require supervised entities to contribute input data to the administrator of a critical benchmark for up to 24 months.

  3. (3)

    The two powers in (a) and (b) above are referred to in this section as the compulsion powers.

Exercise of compulsion powers: general

MAR 8.7.4 G 01/01/2021
  1. (1)

    Articles 21 and 23 of the benchmarks regulation set out the circumstances in which the FCA2 may exercise the compulsion powers.

  2. (2)

    In some cases, the FCA2 may only have a short period in which to decide whether to exercise a compulsion power.

  3. (3)

    Where the FCA considers it necessary to exercise a compulsion power, it will make that decision on the basis of the information available to it at that time.

  4. (4)

    The benchmarks regulation does not require the FCA2 to consult on the use of compulsion powers 2.

  5. (5)

    Given that the compulsion powers may need to be exercised within short timescales, the FCA does not expect to consult on the use of its compulsion powers (other than consulting other regulatory bodies where required by the Act or the benchmarks regulation).

  6. (6)

    In some cases, it may be necessary to exercise compulsion powers in relation to more than one person. In those circumstances, it may be necessary to address a written notice under this section to more than one person.

  7. (7)

    The FCA will review a decision to exercise a compulsion power in the circumstances described in this section.

Decision to exercise a compulsion power

MAR 8.7.5 G 29/06/2018

1If the FCA decides to exercise a compulsion power in respect of a person (P) (whether a supervised entity or an administrator), the FCA will give P a written notice which:

  1. (1)

    gives details of the decision (the First Decision);

  2. (2)

    states the FCAs reasons for the First Decision;

  3. (3)

    states the date on which the First Decision takes effect; and

  4. (4)

    states that P may make representations to the FCA in relation to the First Decision within a period specified in the written notice.

MAR 8.7.6 G 29/06/2018

In some cases, the decision in MAR 8.7.5G may take effect immediately. This means that in some cases:

  1. (1)

    P will be required to comply with the decision from the date of the written notice; and

  2. (2)

    the decision will continue to have effect pending consideration of any representations made by P.

Review of the First Decision

MAR 8.7.7 G 29/06/2018
  1. (1)

    1Where P makes written representations to the FCA in relation to the First Decision in accordance with MAR 8.7.5G(4), the FCA will review that decision and will decide whether to maintain, vary or revoke it.

  2. (2)

    In conducting the review in (1), the matters which the FCA may have regard to include:

    1. (a)

      the written representations made by P in relation to the First Decision; and

    2. (b)

      any additional information relevant to the exercise of the compulsion power (whether obtained before or after the First Decision).

  3. (3)

    The review in (1) will be carried out by:

    1. (a)

      a senior FCA staff member who did not participate in making the First Decision; or

    2. (b)

      two or more senior FCA staff members including at least one person who did not participate in making the First Decision.

  4. (4)

    When the FCA has completed the review in (1), the FCA will give P a written notice which:

    1. (a)

      gives details of the decision in response to the review (the Second Decision);

    2. (b)

      states the FCAs reasons for the Second Decision; and

    3. (c)

      states the date on which the Second Decision takes effect.

Own initiative review of the exercise of compulsion powers

MAR 8.7.8 G 29/06/2018
  1. (1)

    The FCA may, on its own initiative, decide to vary or revoke a requirement imposed under a compulsion power (an Own Initiative Variation or Own Initiative Revocation).

  2. (2)

    For instance, the FCA may decide to vary or revoke a requirement imposed under a compulsion power:

    1. (a)

      where the FCA becomes aware of new information which is material to that requirement; or

    2. (b)

      to extend the duration of the requirement in accordance with article 21(3) or article 23(6)(b) of the benchmarks regulation; or

    3. (c)

      as result of a review under article 21(3) or article 23(9) of the benchmarks regulation.

  3. (3)

    The FCA will treat an Own Initiative Variation as a new First Decision and will follow the procedures in MAR 8.7.5G and MAR 8.7.7G for the purpose of that decision.

MAR 9.2A Consolidated tape providers

Selection of a consolidated tape provider

MAR 9.2A.1G05/04/2024

1The FCA will organise a selection procedure for a single consolidated tape provider for the asset class of bonds, excluding exchange traded commodities and exchange traded notes.

MAR 9.2A.2G05/04/2024

1The FCA will give a direction to tender for the provision of the service of a consolidated tape for bonds by placing a notice on the FCAs website inviting tenders, and providing details of the tender specification and process to be followed. As well as providing additional information to be set out in the direction to tender, tenders should include the information set out in MAR 9.2A.3 and MAR 9.2A.4.

MAR 9.2A.3G05/04/2024
  1. (1)

    1Tenders should include a programme of operations. The programme of operations should include:

    1. (a)

      information on the organisational structure of the bidder, including an organisational chart and a description of the human, technical and legal resources allocated to its business activities;

    2. (b)

      information on the compliance policies and procedures of the bidder, including:

      1. (i)

        the name of the person or persons responsible for the approval and maintenance of those policies;

      2. (ii)

        the arrangements to monitor and enforce the compliance policies and procedures;

      3. (iii)

        the measures to be undertaken in the event of a breach which may result in a failure to meet the conditions for initial authorisation; and

      4. (iv)

        a description of the procedure for reporting to the FCA any breach which may result in a failure to meet the conditions for initial authorisation; and

    3. (c)

      a list of all outsourced functions and resources allocated to the control of the outsourced functions.

  2. (2)

    A bidder offering services other than data reporting services must describe those services in the organisational chart.

MAR 9.2A.4G05/04/2024

1A bidder should include in its tender:

  1. (1)

    a description of the processes for selection, appointment, performance evaluation and removal of senior management and members of the management body;

  2. (2)

    a description of the reporting lines and the frequency of reporting to the senior management and the management body; and

  3. (3)

    a description of the policies and procedures on access to documents by members of the management body.

MAR 9.2A.5G05/04/2024

1After assessing each tender, the FCA will select a single winning bidder by applying the selection criteria in the tender documentation.

MAR 9.2A.6G05/04/2024
  1. (1)

    1The appointment of a CTP for bonds will commence on the date that an authorisation by the FCA of the selected bidder as the CTP for bonds takes effect under regulation 9(4) of the DRS Regulations.

  2. (2)

    The tender contract is awarded for a maximum of 5 years, commencing from a date to be determined by the FCA. The tender contract term is renewable in limited circumstances for a period of no more than 2 years in accordance with regulation 6 of the DRS Regulations.

  3. (3)

    Within 6 months before either the expiry of the tender contract term or the FCA cancelling an authorisation to provide a data reporting service as a CTP under regulation 10 of the DRS Regulations, the FCA will begin the process to re-tender for a CTP for bonds.

MAR 9.2A.7R05/04/2024

1A CTP must publish information relating to its costs for establishing, maintaining and operating the consolidated tape for bonds in such a way as to be accessible to potential bidders in a re-tender process.

MAR 9.2A.8R05/04/2024

1A CTP for bonds must take all reasonable steps to transfer without delay to a successor CTP the assets, data and operational information necessary to enable it to operate the consolidated tape effectively. It must also have in place agreements with its clients which enable those clients who decide to do so to transfer to a successor CTP.
